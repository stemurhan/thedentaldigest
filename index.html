Dental History

Key milestones in dentistry from ancient times to today

This section explores the origins of dental care and the historical advances that shaped modern dentistry. We share archaeological discoveries, ancient practices, and the earliest steps toward root canal therapy.

The earliest known dental intervention

A jawbone from around 7000 BCE discovered in the Italian Alps shows signs of a drilled cavity and a primitive filling. This finding proves dental care dates back thousands of years.

Source: Archaeological studies

Dental care in Ancient Egypt

Ancient Egyptians documented remedies for tooth pain, tooth extractions, and oral hygiene. Several papyri contain dental prescriptions and care instructions.

Source: Ancient medical records

The origins of root canal therapy

The modern concept of root canal treatment emerged in the 19th century. In 1838, Edwin Maynard filled a canal with gutta-percha, providing an early foundation for endodontics.

Source: Endodontic history

The transition to scientific dentistry

Anesthesia, antisepsis, and X-rays in the 18th and 19th centuries transformed dentistry into a modern medical specialty, making procedures safer and more comfortable.

Source: Modern medical history
